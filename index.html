Day 1Saturday 20 June
Arrival

Arrival via Colomares Castle

Your Andalucían adventure begins at Málaga airport. Pick up the rental car and head west on the A-7 — but before continuing to Cádiz, stop at Colomares Castle in Benalmádena, an extraordinary monument built entirely by hand between 1987 and 1994 as a tribute to Christopher Columbus. It blends Gothic, Byzantine and Mudéjar styles into something that looks straight out of a fairy tale — every turret and archway begs to be explored. Inside the grounds is the world's smallest church, at under 2 square metres, listed in the Guinness Book of Records. Allow about an hour, then continue to Cádiz (roughly 1h 45 min). Arrive mid-afternoon, check in, and find somewhere for your first evening tapas.

Aim to arrive before 1pm — the castle closes 2pm–5pm in summer. Entrance €3 adults / €2 children, bought at the door.

Day 2Sunday 21 June
Rest / Beach

Cádiz Beaches & Parque Genovés

A gentle first full day to find your feet. Start at La Caleta, the small beach tucked inside the historic centre — sheltered, compact, and ideal for young children, sitting between two old sea forts. In the afternoon walk to Parque Genovés — one of the most beautiful parks in Andalucía, with a climbable waterfall, shaded paths, a playground and a café. End the day with tapas in the La Viña neighbourhood, Cádiz's liveliest local barrio.

No agenda and no car needed — everything is walkable from the old town.

Day 3Monday 22 June
Excursion

El Puerto de Santa María — Train Out, Boat Back

Leave the car at the hotel and walk to Cádiz train station. The train to El Puerto de Santa María takes just 23 minutes and runs very frequently. Explore the old town, visit the Castillo de San Marcos, and have a long seafood lunch on the Ribera del Marisco — one of the finest strips of fish restaurants in the province. In the afternoon take the catamaran back to Cádiz. As you approach from the water, the city's white skyline appears in a way you simply can't see from the road — a genuinely memorable arrival for the whole family.

Buy the return catamaran ticket on arrival in El Puerto — popular evening crossings sell out. Crossing ~30 min, ~€5 per person.

Day 4Tuesday 23 June
Rest + Meeting · 10:30–11:30

Cádiz Culture Day

After the morning meeting, explore the old town at a relaxed pace. Torre Tavira is the city's 18th-century watchtower and home to a camera obscura that projects a live, moving image of the city onto a curved screen — magical for children. Then the Cathedral: the baroque golden dome is one of the great Andalucían sights, and the tower is climbed by ramp rather than stairs, making it accessible for everyone. The combined ticket covers the tower, crypt and museum.

Book Torre Tavira time slots online in advance — capacity is limited and slots fill quickly in summer.

Day 5Wednesday 24 June
Long Day

Ronda via Arcos de la Frontera

Leave by 8:30am for the most dramatic day of the trip. Stop first at Arcos de la Frontera — a white village perched on a sheer cliff above a gorge, about 30 minutes from Cádiz. Spend an hour exploring its narrow lanes and vertiginous views, then continue to Ronda. The Puente Nuevo bridge over El Tajo gorge is one of the great sights of Andalucía — the drama of looking straight down into the ravine will captivate even the youngest traveller. Walk the old town, visit the Plaza de Toros, and have a long lunch with mountain views. Leave by 4:30pm.

Wednesday is a weekday — far fewer tourists than the weekend. Leave early and return by early evening.

Day 6Thursday 25 June
Rest / Beach

Playa Victoria & Mercado Central

A restorative day after the long drive to Ronda. Start the morning at the Mercado Central de Abastos — Cádiz's lively covered fish market, where the city's restaurants and home cooks do their morning shopping. Gleaming fresh tuna, giant prawns and every variety of local shellfish on ice. Then head to Playa Victoria, the long sandy beach on the south side of the city — wider, calmer and more spacious than La Caleta, with chiringuitos (beach bars) along the front for lunch and cold drinks.

The market is best between 9am and noon. Playa Victoria is a short taxi or walk from the old town.

Day 7Friday 26 June
Rest + Meeting · 13:30–14:30

Old Town Morning & Free Afternoon

A morning to explore El Pópulo — the oldest neighbourhood in Cádiz, a labyrinth of Roman-era lanes tucked behind the Cathedral. Or revisit La Caleta for a morning swim. The meeting falls right in the middle of the day, so keep the pace relaxed on both sides. After the meeting, the afternoon is entirely free — a good opportunity to find a favourite restaurant for a leisurely late lunch, or wander the seafront watching the Atlantic light change.

No driving today — the meeting is in the city centre and everything is on foot.

Day 8Saturday 27 June
Excursion

Jerez de la Frontera

Head to Jerez — the spiritual home of sherry and Andalucían horsemanship. The Royal Andalusian School of Equestrian Art runs morning training sessions on Saturdays: pure white Carthusian horses performing dressage movements in a stunning 19th-century riding hall. Book tickets in advance. Afterwards walk the handsome old town and have lunch. For a sherry bodega, González Byass (Tío Pepe) is the most family-friendly — vast cathedral-like bodegas filled with barrels signed by visiting dignitaries.

Book equestrian school tickets in advance online. The drive from Cádiz is about 45 minutes. Closed Sundays.

Day 9Sunday 28 June
Rest / Beach

Free Day — At Your Own Pace

A recovery day and a chance to do everything at the family's own pace. Revisit whichever beach won the vote, have a long seafood lunch somewhere you've been meaning to try, or take an evening walk along the city walls watching the light change over the Atlantic. Cádiz is at its best when you're not in a hurry — the old city is small enough to wander without a plan, and the evenings are long and warm in late June.

No agenda. Use this day however feels right after the busy week of excursions.

Parque Genovés & Beach Afternoon

A gentle morning around the old town before the meeting. Parque Genovés is right in the city centre — a great spot to let the children run around, with a playground, shaded paths and a climbable waterfall. After the meeting, head to La Caleta or Playa Victoria for a long afternoon on the beach. This is your last proper lazy beach day before the final run of excursions.

Day 11Tuesday 30 June
Excursion

Bolonia Beach & Roman Ruins

Drive south to Baelo Claudia — one of the most complete Roman towns in Spain, with a forum, basilica, temples, theatre and thermal baths, all right on the beach. Free entry for EU residents. Spend 2–3 hours exploring the ruins, then have lunch at one of the beach restaurants. The afternoon is spent on Playa de Bolonia — one of the most beautiful beaches in Andalucía, with enormous Atlantic dunes stretching back from the water and a wild, unspoilt feel quite unlike the town beaches.

Closed Mondays — Tuesday is correct. Drive ~1 hour from Cádiz. Free entry for EU passport holders.

Day 12Wednesday 1 July
Excursion

Seville

Leave by 8:30am for the grandest day of the trip. Start with the Real Alcázar — a royal palace of extraordinary Moorish architecture, with elaborate gardens, fountains and tiled courtyards. Book tickets well in advance — July queues can be very long. Afterwards walk through the Barrio de Santa Cruz for lunch among whitewashed lanes and orange trees. In the afternoon, the Cathedral and Giralda tower (climbed by ramp) rewards with panoramic city views. Leave by 4:30–5pm.

Wednesday is the best weekday — fewest crowds mid-week. Consider the train from Cádiz (hourly, ~1h 40 min) to avoid parking. Book Alcázar well in advance.

Day 13Thursday 2 July
Departure

Departure — Cádiz to Málaga Airport

Check out and drive back to Málaga Airport — approximately 2 hours. In summer, leave by 9am at the latest to be comfortable for most morning or midday flights. Return the rental car at the terminal and depart. If time allows, a final coffee and pastry on the seafront in the old town makes a perfect send-off.

Allow extra time for summer traffic on the A-7/AP-4. Leave no later than 9am.
