What to Expect

This itinerary balances magical cultural moments (gondolas, glassblowing, Renaissance palaces) with plenty of downtime, water play, and family-friendly attractions. Every other day at Lake Garda is a "camp day" where you rest, swim, and enjoy the on-site facilities. The alternate days are short car excursions (30 min–1 hour) to nearby attractions.

📍 Geography Note: The campsite is on Lake Garda (Veneto region), not near Tuscany. But Lake Garda is arguably better for families — it has excellent infrastructure, stunning scenery, and tons of kid-friendly day trips.
🛶 Venice (Aug 1–4)

Three magical days to explore the city of canals. Arrival on August 1; transfer to Lake Garda on August 4 morning.

Day 1 — Arrival (August 1)
CPH → Venice | Flight SK2691
Land at Venice Marco Polo Airport (VCE). Take the Alilaguna water bus (€15/person, 75 min) directly to your Airbnb in Dorsoduro, 2633. Settle in, rest, then wander to a nearby bacaro (wine bar) for your first Venetian dinner.
🏠 Your Airbnb: Dorsoduro, 2633, Venice, Veneto 30125 — In the artsy Dorsoduro neighbourhood, 10 min walk to Accademia Bridge and St. Mark's.

Pro Tips

  • Book Alilaguna tickets online beforehand
  • Shoulder and knees must be covered for St. Mark's — bring scarves
  • Stay in Dorsoduro or Cannaregio for quieter neighborhoods
Book water bus
Day 2 — Grand Canal & St. Mark's (August 2)
🛶 Venice exploration
Start with a vaporetto (water bus) ride down the Grand Canal — 45 minutes of pure magic. Watch gondolas, bridges, and Renaissance palaces glide by. Then visit the glittering St. Mark's Basilica (book timed entry online!) and climb the Campanile bell tower for lake and rooftop views. Afternoon: wander Dorsoduro's quiet canals and the Accademia Bridge.

Must-See

  • Vaporetto Line 1, Grand Canal (full 45-min route)
  • St. Mark's Basilica — golden mosaics, stunning interior
  • Campanile bell tower — 100m views over the city
  • Accademia Bridge sunset photo
Full day Venice

Day 3 — Murano & Burano Islands (August 3)
🎨 Glass blowing & rainbow houses
Ferry to Murano (Line 4.1, ~15 min) and book a glass-blowing workshop. Watch master craftsmen shape molten glass into stunning pieces — kids can often make their own mosaic tile or bracelet. Then ferry to Burano, the island of impossibly colourful houses. Lunch at a trattoria. Return by mid-afternoon; optional sunset gondola ride (€80 per gondola, magical!).

Activity Highlights

  • Murano glass-blowing demonstration (book in advance!)
  • Hands-on workshop — make your own Murano piece
  • Burano's rainbow houses — Instagram gold for the 12-year-old
  • Optional: Gondola ride at sunset

Day 4 — Transfer to Lake Garda (August 4)
🚗 Venice → Bardolino
Morning: explore the Rialto Market (go before 11am for the full bustle). Pick up your rental car from Piazzale Roma or Tronchetto. Drive ~2 hours along the A4 motorway to Camping Cisano/San Vito on Lake Garda. Check in, discover the lagoon pools and slides, have pizza with a lake view.
🚗 Car Rental Pickup: 30 Viale Galileo Galilei, Venice — Near Tronchetto car park area.

Logistics

  • Rialto Market open early, closes by 11am
  • Rent car evening of Aug 3 to save time
  • A4 motorway direct to Bardolino (~2 hrs)
  • Campsite has restaurants, beach, pools

🏕️ Lake Garda (Aug 4–15)

11 nights at Camping Cisano/San Vito. Alternating camp days (rest, swim, pools) with day trips to nearby attractions.

Day 5 — Camp Day (August 5)
🌊 Beach, pools & Bardolino walk
Settle in at the campsite. The 700m sandy beach (rare on Lake Garda!) is perfect for sunbathing, building sandcastles, and swimming. There are two lagoon pools with waterslides — the 6-year-old will love them. Hire a paddleboard or pedal boat on the lake. Evening: walk the lakeside path to nearby Bardolino village (~40 min) for ice cream and sunset views.

Day 6 — Verona: Romeo, Juliet & the Arena (August 6)
🚗 ~40 min drive | Ancient Roman Amphitheatre
Leave early to beat August heat. Park at Piazzale Kipling. Visit the Arena di Verona (30 AD Roman amphitheatre, one of the world's best-preserved). Kids can walk the ancient stone tiers. Find Juliet's House and the balcony — the 12-year-old will love it. Walk across the stunning Ponte Scaligero (14th-century fortified bridge). Lunch at a piazza restaurant. Back by late afternoon for a lake swim.

Highlights

  • Arena di Verona — walk ancient seating, amazing scale
  • Casa di Giulietta — courtyard free, photo op essential
  • Ponte Scaligero — free, gorgeous river views
  • Go early! August crowds are intense by midday

Day 7 — Camp Day: Gardaland! (August 7)
🎢 Italy's largest theme park (10 min away!)
Gardaland is literally 10 minutes from the campsite — Italy's biggest amusement park with 40+ attractions, 7 roller coasters, Peppa Pig Land for the 6-year-old, and high-thrill rides for the 12-year-old. Dynamic pricing: buy online in advance, the earlier the cheaper. Arrive at opening (10am) to beat queues. Bring sunscreen and your own water bottle (food inside is pricey).

Gardaland Facts

  • 40+ attractions for all ages
  • Gate price: €56 adult, €50 child | Online from €44
  • Kids under 90cm enter FREE
  • Book online, never at gate
  • Full day needed to see everything
Camp day activity

Day 8 — Sirmione: Medieval Castle (August 8)
🚗 ~35 min drive | Fairytale fortress
Sirmione sits on a peninsula jutting into southern Lake Garda. Park outside the historic centre (car-free zone), walk across the drawbridge into the 13th-century Scaligero Castle — surrounded entirely by water! Kids love climbing the towers and walking the battlements. Visit the Grotte di Catullo (Roman ruins at the peninsula's tip, with swimming access). Lunch at a waterfront restaurant. Alternatively, take the ferry from Bardolino directly — kids love the boat ride!

Sirmione Highlights

  • Scaligero Castle — cross the moat, climb the towers
  • Free entry for under-18s; adults ~€8
  • Grotte di Catullo — largest Roman ruins in north Italy
  • Sandy/rocky beaches for swimming

Day 9 — Camp Day (August 9)
🌊 Lake & Lazise village
Full lazy beach day. Use the campsite's sandy beach, rent a kayak or paddleboard, swim. No activities, just relaxation. Evening: walk (or cycle) 2km south to Lazise — a medieval harbour village far less touristy than Bardolino. Explore the intact town walls, watch fishing boats, have dinner overlooking the water.

Day 10 — Malcesine & Monte Baldo Cable Car (August 10)
🚗 ~40 min drive | Rotating cabin to 1,760m
Drive to Malcesine, a picturesque town on the eastern shore. The highlight: the Monte Baldo cable car. This unique lift takes you 1,760m up in just 20 minutes. The second-section cabin slowly rotates 360° — everyone gets unobstructed panoramic views of the entire lake, the Po Valley, and the Alps. It's genuinely awe-inspiring even for jaded teenagers. At the top: alpine meadows, easy walks, cooler temperatures. Book your time slot online beforehand to avoid 1+ hour queues.

Monte Baldo Facts

  • 20-minute ascent with rotating cabin (360° views!)
  • Round-trip: ~€25–30 per person
  • Kids under 1.20m often ride FREE
  • Much cooler at top — bring a layer
  • Book time slot online in advance (queues brutal in Aug)

Day 12 — Mantua: Hidden Renaissance Gem (August 12)
🚗 ~55 min drive | UNESCO city, your longest day trip
This is your one longer excursion — and it's absolutely worth it. Mantua is a UNESCO World Heritage city on three lakes, far less touristy than Verona. Visit the Palazzo Ducale (Gonzaga palace) with 500 rooms and stunning frescoed chambers — Mantegna's Camera degli Sposi is jaw-dropping. Walk the beautiful Piazza Sordello and Piazza delle Erbe. Lunch is essential here: try tortelli di zucca (pumpkin pasta), risotto alla pilota, and sbrisolona (crumbly almond cake). Afternoon: rent a rowboat on the lakes for a peaceful perspective of the city walls.

Mantua Highlights

  • Palazzo Ducale — 500 rooms, incredible Renaissance art
  • Timed entry recommended in August
  • Free entry for under-18s; adults ~€15
  • Food is WORLD-CLASS — don't skip lunch
  • Row boat rental on the lakes

Day 14 — Lake Ferry & Western Shore (August 14)
🚗 & 🚢 Car ferry adventure
A beautiful final adventure. Drive to Garda town (20 min away), explore its narrow cobbled streets. Then drive to nearby Torri del Benaco and take the car ferry across the lake to Toscolano-Maderno on the western shore (~30 min crossing). Kids LOVE being on a car ferry in the middle of a lake! Explore Gargnano (one of the lake's most beautiful, least touristy towns). Lunch with views back to the Alps. Drive back via the scenic south shore. Final lake sunset swim at camp.

Ferry Details

  • Torri del Benaco–Toscolano ferry runs every 30–60 min
  • ~€15 for car + 4 passengers
  • Arrive 15 min early to queue the car
  • Check timetable: navigazionelaghi.it

📅 Planning & Practical Info

Must-Book in Advance

  • St. Mark's Basilica — since July 2025, there are ZERO walk-up tickets. Book at tickets.basilicasanmarco.it
  • Gardaland — dynamic pricing; book online weeks ahead for best rates
  • Monte Baldo cable car — time-slot booking essential (avoid 1+ hour queues)
  • Murano glassblowing workshop — fills up fast in high season
  • Palazzo Ducale Mantua — timed entry recommended in August

Packing Tips for August in Italy

  • Sun protection: Strong sunscreen (SPF 50+), hats, sunglasses, rash guards for swimming
  • Clothing: Light, breathable fabrics; long sleeves for sun protection; one layer for air conditioning
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes for Venice (lots of walking, lots of bridges); water shoes for beach/pools
  • Other: Reusable water bottle, lightweight sarong/cover-up, medications, small first-aid kit
  • Documentation: Passports, travel insurance, booking confirmations (print or screenshot)

Restaurant & Food Tips

Venice: Avoid restaurants directly on St. Mark's Square (tourist trap). Walk 5 min into the alleys — better food, lower prices.
Lake Garda: The campsite has on-site restaurants (pizzeria, restaurants). Local trattorias in Bardolino and Lazise are excellent and family-friendly.
Mantua: One of Italy's great food cities. Try tortelli di zucca, risotto alla pilota. Don't miss Osteria dell'Oca or Trattoria dei Marchesi.
